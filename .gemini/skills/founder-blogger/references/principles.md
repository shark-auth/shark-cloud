# The "High Ceiling" Principles

In an era of AI-generated "cheap cupcakes," your writing must target the ceiling. These three principles ensure your posts provide information only you can know.

## 1. The Why Over Everything
Customers don't care about features; they care about the strategic bets you are making on the future.
- **Goal:** Connect every update to a thesis or strategic claim.
- **The Test:** If you swap your brand for a competitor's and it still reads fine, it's a "grocery store cupcake."
- **Focus:** Show the insight or secret that sparked the build.

## 2. Have an Opinion
Good products are built off "spiky opinions." Product announcements should be the same.
- **Goal:** Make an argument. Defend a point of view.
- **Method:** Avoid committee-softened language. Don't hedge. 
- **Voice:** Write with a point of view you are willing to defend.

## 3. Show What You Chose Not to Build
Walking users through hard trade-offs creates a human story.
- **Goal:** Reveal the decision process.
- **Method:** List the features or ideas you *rejected*. Explain why.
- **Impact:** It proves you are human and making deliberate choices, not just executing a roadmap.
